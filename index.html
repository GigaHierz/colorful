Who are we?

We are a group of three students participating in a Citizen Science course at the University of Leipzig. Citizen Science is the field of scientific research undertaken by the public and allows for science to be more accessible by including the ideas of the public rather than focusing on a kind of academic elitism.

We need You!

That is why we are reaching out to you: we would value your opinion and contribution to how we could best implement certain aspects of our project. We want to include as much of the community as possible.

What is this Project about?

Our project is inspired by the events of the last few months, especially the Black Live Matters movement. Racially-charged experiences and confrontations are becoming more prominently shared through various types of media. These experiences that we are seeing on an almost daily basis have captured attention on a global stage. More and more people are sharing their experiences through a variety of mediums on a variety of platforms. The goal of our project is to curate an online collection of artistic mediums focusing on racially-charged encounters to be used as a means to educate, as well as to build a community where people feel that they can share their own experiences and be heard and seen. We want to build a centralized space that focuses on this topic in particular.

Our Priciples

Community safety is a big concern for us. In the age of internet trolls, it is very easy for a post or a page to become overrun with content that detracts from and derails the issues. For this reason, only registered users will be allowed to post or comment. All contributions will be reviewed, and comments with racist language will be prevented by having a running list of unacceptable, racially-charged language. A code of conduct will be drafted to help guide appropriate behaviour within the community. Sensitive topics will also be indicated before a user can view the post. In the beginning, this will be manually reviewed and will take into consideration the desires of the community.

In terms of user security, we want to ensure that each user feels comfortable with the data they are sharing with us. A user will need an email address to register, and this information will be encrypted. A registered user has the option to share their demographic data but is not required to do so. We want to have as little obligatory information as possible. Collected data might potentially be sent to educational reasearch institutions ( no private organizations) that focus on anti-discrimnation studies, but beyond that data shall not be sold to any third-parties.

We want to build the app in a decentralized way. Meaning all your presonal data will be stored in your phone and you can decide if and when to share it with people you connect with. But we wont store your information in a centralized space. Which gives you more power and controll over your data.

Our goal would be to create this platform for and with the community. So we will be putting a lot of effort into making the app as accessible and sustainable as possible. We would like to make the mobile app very bandwith friendly. We are planning to offer the app with multiple possible translations, that can also be added by the community. The standard for the app should be that it is accesssible to everyone. If you want to help with the translations please contact us or go to https://github.colorful.translations.com and add upload your translation.

We would also like to include a section that offers further research on different aspects of racism, as well as historical events and figures.

Citizens Scence Approach

The main idea behind the project is the educational and community building approach. But the collected cotribution can be used in the future as a data base for anti-discrimintaion studies.

Another approach would be to create a database for training a machine learning algorithm that decets racism and hate speach to filters these comments. We would like to write something like this into our code of conduct: “If you use hate speach or dicrimating language, you are actively participating to train our machine learning algorihtm to detect this.”
